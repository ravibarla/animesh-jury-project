INDIAN CRAFT WORKS

Chikankari


Chikankari is a very delicate and complex type of shadow embroidery. Initially, embroidery was performed on colorless muslin known as tanzeb with white thread. But today, georgette, chiffon, cotton and other premium fabrics are also used. Chikankari embroidery in India extends from ornaments mainly used for clothing decoration to cushion covers, cushion covers, table linen and so on.

Leather Craft


In India, the tradition of leather craftsmanship dates back to the Indus Valley civilization. There are some references to the use of animal skin in ancient texts. The ancient tradition of Indian leather craft is evidenced by ancient sages and asceticists who sit on deer skin for meditation and other similar tasks. In the past, leather was used not only to make clothes and shoes, but also to make hats, bags, saddles and armor. India is known worldwide for its leather goods. In rural India, cow and camel skins are used locally to dry, tan and then make a variety of things.

Metal Craft


The art of metalworking has been known to Indians for almost 5,000 years. The beautiful image of Mohenjo-daro dancers proves this fact. This shows the high level of craftsmanship of ancient craftsmen. Traditionally, Indian craftsmen used various metals such as iron, copper and silver, and alloys such as bronze, bell metal and white metal to pots, frying pans, utensils, photo frames, and divine sculptures.
