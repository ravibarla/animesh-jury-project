ABOUT BLUE POTTERY


Jaipur's blue pottery is literally famous nationwide and even around the world. The artwork is blue because the pottery has a blue color made of blue dye, combined with an eye-catching gold and silver design, and the art style is actually derived from the Turco Persian style. It is called pottery. The blue color used to color the pottery is actually by Egyptian techniques using Indian raw materials such as Multani MItti, Fuller's Earth Borax, Katira Gond, or a type of gum, regular gum, Saaji. The color obtained. Baking soda and water. In addition to coloring, the pottery does not use clay, so it is made from Egyptian faience grilled over low heat and glazed frit material. Quartz stone powder and glass powder are used for pottery dough instead of ordinary clay. Jaipur's blue pottery is easily recognizable in the local market, and no one can discourage them by seeing the beautiful motifs often found in animal motifs such as birds, horses and camels in blue and gold colors. This pottery can be purchased in a variety of forms, including vases, ashtrays, jugs, cups, tea sets, small bowls and tableware. However, they are fragile and should be handled with great care.
